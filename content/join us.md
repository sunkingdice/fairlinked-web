## If you are a professional LinkedIn user

Fairlinked conducts independent research on how LinkedIn works and maintains an ongoing regulatory dialog with EU institutions. This gives us deep insight into LinkedIn's technical infrastructure, its algorithm, and industry data that is not publicly available.

We share these findings with our members. We also advise on legal and data protection issues and provide access to documents and research that come out of our work.

<p>&nbsp;</p>

## If you are a business that depends on LinkedIn

Your company invests in LinkedIn through advertising, recruiting, and sales tools. You have a right to fair access, transparent pricing, and non-discriminatory treatment. Fairlinked advocates for these rights at the regulatory level and tracks how LinkedIn's policies affect businesses that rely on the platform.

When LinkedIn changes its rules, restricts access, or degrades services, we document it, report it, and push back.

<p>&nbsp;</p>

## If you build tools for LinkedIn

Fairlinked runs a Working Group for tool makers who want to shape how LinkedIn's APIs, data structures, and access procedures are designed. This is not observer access. Members have full speaking rights, can propose amendments to API white papers and regulatory submissions, and have direct input on the technical demands submitted to LinkedIn and the EU Commission.

The Working Group is limited to 10 members and requires an NDA. If you build products that depend on the LinkedIn platform, this is where the rules are being written.

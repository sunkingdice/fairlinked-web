# Listen, Don't Write

## Purpose
The best-performing messages come from your customers, not from your head. Stop trying to write and start listening to how your customers actually talk about your solutions.

## Core Principle
Your best messages are not waiting inside your head. They're not in your CEO's brain. They can't be discovered in a boardroom. They're in the words your customers and prospects already use.

## Where to Find Customer Language

### 1. Testimonials
Look for specific phrases customers use to describe benefits and outcomes.

### 2. Customer Reviews
Find the exact words people use when reviewing your product or service.

### 3. Support Emails
Identify how customers describe their problems and your solutions.

### 4. Surveys
Extract the language customers use when answering questions.

### 5. Social Media Comments
Capture how people talk about your brand, products, or industry.

### 6. Competitor Reviews
See how people describe similar solutions (even if they're talking about competitors).

## How to Use Customer Language

### For Headlines
**Bad (written by you):** "The Only Small Business Compliance Suite That Works The Way You Do"

**Good (from customer testimonial):** "Eliminate Up to 99% Of Your Paper Files"

### For Body Copy
Take customer phrases and use them verbatim or adapt them slightly for flow.

**Example from testimonial:**
- "This tool saved me 10 hours per week on compliance tracking"

**Use in copy:**
- "Save 10 hours per week on compliance tracking with our automated system"

### For Social Media Posts
Use customer language to make posts more relatable and authentic.

**Example:**
- Instead of: "Our solution helps with TTPA compliance"
- Use: "Finally, a tool that makes TTPA compliance actually manageable" (from customer review)

## When to Use

### Blog Posts
- Use customer language in headlines, subheads, and body copy
- Quote customers directly when possible
- Adapt customer phrases for section headings

### Social Media Posts
- Use customer language in post copy
- Quote customers in posts
- Repurpose customer reviews as social proof

### Glossary Entries
- Use customer language to explain terms in relatable ways
- Include customer examples that use natural language

## Pro Tip: Frequency Matters

Listen not just to the words customers use, but the frequency with which they talk about specific benefits or outcomes. The most frequently mentioned benefits are likely your strongest selling points.

## Key Takeaway

When you stop trying to write and start listening to your customers, your copy writes itself. The best-performing messages are those swiped directly from testimonials, reviews, support emails, surveys, and anywhere your prospects are talking about solutions like yours.

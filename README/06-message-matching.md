# Message Matching

## Purpose
When readers arrive from another source (email, social post, ad), your content should match the message and expectations that brought them there.

## Core Principle
Your content should both:
1. Match the message of the source that drove readers
2. Describe what's uniquely desirable about your offering

This creates a Message-Matched Unique Selling Proposition (MMUSP).

## How Message Matching Works

### Example 1: Search to Content
**Search query:** "lose weight"

**Ad headline:** "How to lose weight fast"

**Content headline should include:**
- **Message Match (MM):** "How to lose weight fast"
- **Unique Selling Proposition (USP):** "The 'ideal protein' protocol combined with professional weight loss coaching"

**Result:** "How to Lose Weight Fast: The Ideal Protein Protocol with Professional Coaching"

### Example 2: Email to Blog Post
**Email subject:** "Personalization is the Key to Post-Holiday Campaigns"

**Blog headline should include:**
- **Message Match (MM):** "Personalization is the key to post-holiday campaigns"
- **Unique Selling Proposition (USP):** "We have data that no one else has, and if you get access to that data, you can use it to keep making money after the holidays have ended"

**Result:** "Personalization is the Key to Post-Holiday Campaigns: Access Exclusive Data to Extend Your Revenue"

## When to Use

### Blog Posts
- Match headlines to the source that drove traffic (email, social, search)
- Include both the expected message AND your unique value
- Don't match words verbatim if phrasing is awkward—adapt naturally

### Social Media Posts
- Match post content to the platform's context
- If linking to a blog post, match the social post message to the blog headline
- Ensure consistency across the journey

### Glossary Entries
- Match entry titles to common search queries
- Include both the term (message match) and a unique angle (USP) if applicable

## Pro Tip: Don't Match Verbatim

You don't have to match the words exactly. If exact matching creates awkward phrasing, adapt it naturally while maintaining the core message.

**Example:**
- **Source:** "TTPA compliance made easy"
- **Awkward verbatim:** "TTPA Compliance Made Easy: Here's How"
- **Better adaptation:** "TTPA Compliance Made Simple: A Step-by-Step Guide"

## Key Takeaway

Match the expectations your reader brings to the content by specifically stating what fantastic, high-value outcome they're going to get from you. Combine message matching with your unique selling proposition to create compelling, relevant content.

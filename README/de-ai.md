
In order to make a good copy look and feel like its human and not AI generated we need to remove the AI smell:

Systematically replace em-dashes (“—”) with a dot (”.”) to start a new sentence, or a comma (”,”) to continue the sentence.

How to spot AI written posts 100% of the time:  
  
-It’s not X. It’s Y. Stop X. Start Y.  
(Contradiction play to sound smart)  

-Real stories spill. AI scripts glide.  
(Contrastive parallels)  

-That X? Its..  
(The Bait & Flip)  

-Here is the kicker:  
(Here is the X: followed by bullets)  

-I was devastated.  
(Heavy ornamental words without any real emotion)  

-3 Words: separated.by.points.  
(Three words. Separated. By points. - Although I do write this sometimes)  

-Same length of bullets  
(“AI killed your reach: here’s why.” - AI loves Colons)  

-Ending a post with a weird question  
 (Forced “engagement”)  
 
-Use of em dash  
(Humans spill — AI aligns)  

-Certain words: harness, supercharge, unleash, delve, catalyst  
(If it sounds like a marketing thesaurus, it probably is)  

-"The Title"  
("The AI" - It loves to make titles with colons)  

I can tell when you've used ChatGPT to write your post.  
  
It's not even hard to spot anymore.  
  
The telltale signs are everywhere:  
  
"Let's dive in..."  
"Here's the thing..."  
"At the end of the day..."  
"Let's unpack this..."  
  
Nobody actually talks like that. But AI bloody loves it.  
  
Then there's the structure.  
Always the same.  
  
Bold opening statement. Three bullet points. Inspiring conclusion that sounds like a motivational poster.  
  
Every. Single. Time.  
  
The worst part is that the posts always feel... polished, but empty.  
Technically correct but weirdly soulless.  
  
Like someone asked a robot to write something engaging and it produced the most generic version of engaging possible.  
  
I'm not saying don't use AI. I use it myself for loads of stuff. It's a tool, and tools are useful.  
  
But if you're just copying and pasting whatever ChatGPT spits out without adding any of yourself to it... why bother posting at all?  
  
Your audience can tell. Maybe they don't know exactly what's off about it, but they can feel it. That's why those posts get polite likes but zero real engagement.  
  
No comments. No discussion. Just people scrolling past thinking "yeah, fine, whatever."  
  
If you're going to use AI to help with your content, at least make it sound like you. Edit it. Add your personality. Chuck in a swear word or two if that's your thing.  
  
Make it messy. Make it human.

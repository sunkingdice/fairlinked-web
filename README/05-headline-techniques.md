# Headline Techniques

## Purpose
Headlines have one job: keep arriving readers on the page (or keep them reading). Use these techniques to write headlines that do that job effectively.

## Core Principle
Your headline's job is to keep readers reading. It doesn't need to summarize everything, express your value proposition, rank for keywords, and convince people to act—that's too much. Just keep them reading.

## The "Even If" Technique

Add "even if" to address objections and make your headline more compelling.

**Bad:** "We'll help you settle with the IRS out of court"

**Good:** "We'll help you settle with the IRS out of court even if you owe thousands of dollars and are too worried to sleep"

**Application:**
- Blog posts: "TTPA Compliance Made Simple Even If You're Starting from Scratch"
- Social posts: "You Can Achieve TTPA Compliance Even If You Have Zero Legal Experience"

## The Open Loop Technique

Give readers the first half of important information, with the promise of closing the loop in the content.

**Example:**
- **Email/Ad:** "The one TTPA mistake that costs companies €50K..."
- **Headline:** "...and how to avoid it in 3 simple steps"

**Application:**
- Blog posts: Use in the opening to create curiosity
- Social posts: Start with an intriguing statement, complete it in the post

## Match the Message That Brought Them

If readers arrive from an email, social post, or ad, your headline should reflect the message that compelled them to click.

**Example:**
- **Email subject:** "How to avoid TTPA penalties"
- **Blog headline:** "How to Avoid TTPA Penalties: A Step-by-Step Guide"

## When to Use

### Blog Posts
- Use "even if" to address common objections
- Use open loops in series or multi-part content
- Match headlines to the source that drove traffic

### Social Media Posts
- Use "even if" in post openings to address skepticism
- Use open loops in thread-style posts
- Match post openings to the platform's context

### Glossary Entries
- Use clear, direct headlines that match search intent
- Avoid "even if" in glossary (too promotional)
- Focus on clarity and directness

## Pro Tip: Test Headline Variations

Test different headline approaches:
- Benefit-focused: "Save 10 Hours Per Week on TTPA Compliance"
- Problem-focused: "The TTPA Mistake That Costs Companies €50K"
- Question-based: "Are You Making These 5 TTPA Compliance Mistakes?"
- "Even if" approach: "TTPA Compliance Made Simple Even If You're Overwhelmed"

## Key Takeaway

Your headline has one job: keep readers reading. Don't overload it with multiple responsibilities. Use techniques like "even if" and open loops to make it more compelling, but always remember its core purpose.

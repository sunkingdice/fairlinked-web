# Closing Hooks (Endings)

## Purpose
Closing hooks drive action and leave readers with a clear next step. They reinforce your message and convert engagement into action.

**IMPORTANT: These endings are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles or glossary articles.**

## Core Principle
Your ending's job is to move readers to action. It should match the emotional journey you started with your opening hook and provide a clear, compelling call to action.

**Content Type Restriction:**
- **Do use:** Social media posts (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- **Don't use:** Journal articles, blog posts, glossary entries, or any long-form content

## Quick Decision Guide

### By Content Goal
- **Motivate action** → Use MOTIVATE or POSITIVE OUTCOME
- **Sell product/service** → Use SELL
- **Engage audience** → Use ENGAGE
- **Show consequences** → Use NEGATIVE OUTCOME
- **Challenge thinking** → Use PROVOKE
- **Summarize key points** → Use SUMMARIZE

### By Reader State
- **Ready to act** → Use SELL or MOTIVATE
- **Needs encouragement** → Use POSITIVE OUTCOME or MOTIVATE
- **Needs warning** → Use NEGATIVE OUTCOME
- **Needs recap** → Use SUMMARIZE
- **Wants to engage** → Use ENGAGE

### By Emotional Tone
- **Positive/aspirational** → MOTIVATE, POSITIVE OUTCOME
- **Sales-focused** → SELL
- **Warning/consequence** → NEGATIVE OUTCOME
- **Contrarian** → PROVOKE
- **Educational** → SUMMARIZE
- **Conversational** → ENGAGE

## The 7 Ending Categories

### 1. MOTIVATE

**What It Is:**
Endings that inspire and motivate readers to take action. Creates positive energy and encourages forward movement.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- When readers need encouragement
- For aspirational social media content
- After inspirational hooks
- When you want to create momentum

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- When urgency is needed (use SELL instead)
- For problem-focused content (use NEGATIVE OUTCOME)
- When readers need specific next steps (use SELL)

**Template Patterns:**

1. `It's time to stand out from the crowd. [PROVIDE A CHALLENGE OR INSPIRATION]. By [DOING X], you'll [ACHIEVE Y DIFFERENTIATION].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[PROVIDE A CHALLENGE OR INSPIRATION]`, `[DOING X]`, `[ACHIEVE Y DIFFERENTIATION]`
   - **Example:** "It's time to stand out from the crowd. Develop a niche in social selling coaching for B2B companies. By focusing on a specific audience, you'll establish yourself as an expert and attract high-value clients."

2. `Seize the opportunity to [DO X]. Your [GOAL Y] depends on it.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DO X]`, `[GOAL Y]`
   - **Example:** "Seize the opportunity to find the best lawyer for your company. Your future success and legal protection depend on it."

3. `Every journey begins with a single step. Start [DOING X] today and pave the way for [ACHIEVING Y OUTCOME].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DOING X]`, `[ACHIEVING Y OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "Every journey begins with a single step. Start investing in employee training and development today and pave the way for your IT outsourcing company's long-term success."

4. `The time is now. [STATE A BENEFIT OF X]. [END WITH A MOTIVATIONAL PHRASE].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[STATE A BENEFIT OF X]`, `[END WITH A MOTIVATIONAL PHRASE]`
   - **Example:** "The time is now. Taking control of your finances is liberating. Your financial freedom awaits."

5. `It's time to take action. [PROVIDE A CLEAR CALL-TO-ACTION]. By [DOING X], you'll [ACHIEVE Y BENEFIT].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[PROVIDE A CLEAR CALL-TO-ACTION]`, `[DOING X]`, `[ACHIEVE Y BENEFIT]`
   - **Example:** "It's time to take action. Start engaging with your LinkedIn network. By leaving thoughtful comments and sharing valuable content, you'll build strong relationships and increase your visibility on the platform."

6. `Don't let this opportunity slip away. [MAKE A STRONG RECOMMENDATION]. [DOING X] can help you [ACHIEVE Y RESULT].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[MAKE A STRONG RECOMMENDATION]`, `[DOING X]`, `[ACHIEVE Y RESULT]`
   - **Example:** "Don't let this opportunity slip away. Start using LinkedIn groups to your advantage. It helps you build relationships, gain credibility, and increase brand visibility."

7. `You're closer than you think. [STATE A BARRIER TO X]. [STATE HOW TO OVERCOME THAT BARRIER].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[STATE A BARRIER TO X]`, `[STATE HOW TO OVERCOME THAT BARRIER]`
   - **Example:** "You're closer than you think. Building a successful IT outsourcing company can seem daunting. Focus on niche specialization and exceptional customer service to overcome the challenge."

8. `Believe in yourself and [DO X]. You have the power to [ACHIEVE Y GOAL].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DO X]`, `[ACHIEVE Y GOAL]`
   - **Example:** "Believe in yourself and prioritize client-centric marketing strategies. You have the power to revolutionize the way your marketing agency operates."

9. `Don't miss out on [BENEFIT RELATED TO X]. [TRY/LEARN X] today and take your [GOAL RELATED TO X] to the next level.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[BENEFIT RELATED TO X]`, `[TRY/LEARN X]`, `[GOAL RELATED TO X]`
   - **Example:** "Don't miss out on potential clients and business opportunities. Learn how to effectively use LinkedIn Sales Navigator today and take your lead generation to the next level."

**How to Adapt:**
- Use empowering language
- Focus on positive outcomes
- Create a sense of possibility
- Make action feel achievable

### 2. SELL

**What It Is:**
Endings that directly promote a product, service, or offer. Includes clear calls to action and next steps.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- When promoting a product or service on social media
- For sales-focused social media content
- After problem-focused hooks
- When readers are ready to buy

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- For educational content (too salesy)
- When building trust first
- For cold audiences (too pushy)

**Template Patterns:**

1. `Looking to level up your [SKILL/KNOWLEDGE]? Grab my free [RESOURCE] on [TOPIC]. Get it here: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[SKILL/KNOWLEDGE]`, `[RESOURCE]`, `[TOPIC]`, `[LINK]`

2. `Want to learn more? Check out my latest blog post/podcast episode on [TOPIC]. I'm sure you'll find it valuable! Read/listen here: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[TOPIC]`, `[LINK]`
   - **Example:** "P.S. Want to learn more about the future of software architecture? Check out my latest blog post on 'The Rise of Serverless Architecture.' I'm sure you'll find it valuable! Read here: [LINK]"

3. `P.S. Ready to take your [SKILL/GOAL] to the next level? Our [PRODUCT/SERVICE] has helped [NUMBER] of professionals achieve [BENEFIT]. Don't miss out! Check it out here: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[SKILL/GOAL]`, `[PRODUCT/SERVICE]`, `[NUMBER]`, `[BENEFIT]`, `[LINK]`
   - **Example:** "P.S. Ready to take your public speaking skills to the next level? Our proven Public Speaking Mastery course has helped over 1,000 professionals confidently deliver their message and land more opportunities. Don't miss out! Check it out here: [LINK]"

4. `P.S. Want to see results like [CUSTOMER AND THEIR RESULT]? Our [PRODUCT/SERVICE] can help you get there too. [CTA]. Link: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[CUSTOMER AND THEIR RESULT]`, `[PRODUCT/SERVICE]`, `[CTA]`, `[LINK]`
   - **Example:** "Want to see results like Sarah, who increased her sales by 30% in just one month? Our sales training program can help you get there too. Don't wait - enroll now!"

5. `P.S. I currently have [X SPOTS] open for [SPECIFIC SERVICE]. If you want to work together, send a connection request or message with the word "[KEYWORD]" in it. [MINIMUM REQUIREMENT].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X SPOTS]`, `[SPECIFIC SERVICE]`, `[KEYWORD]`, `[MINIMUM REQUIREMENT]`

6. `If you think my [SERVICE/PRODUCT] could be a great fit for you, send me a [SPECIFIC WORD] in a direct message, and let's chat about how we can work together!`

   - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVE GOAL]`, `[PRODUCT/SERVICE]`, `[UNIQUE FEATURE]`, `[BENEFIT]`, `[PAIN POINT]`, `[DREAM RESULT]`
   - **Example:** "P.S. Want to grow your personal brand like a pro? Our LinkedIn Brand Building service has an exclusive 1-on-1 coaching feature that can help you create and promote your brand effectively. Say goodbye to guesswork and hello to an engaged, captive audience! Send me a DM to discuss."

8. `P.S. Want to [GOAL RELATED TO X]? Our [COURSE/PROGRAM] is designed to help you [BENEFIT] in just [X] weeks. Check it out here: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[GOAL RELATED TO X]`, `[COURSE/PROGRAM]`, `[BENEFIT]`, `[X]`, `[LINK]`

9. `P.S. Interested in staying up-to-date with [TOPIC/TRENDS]? Subscribe to my newsletter for weekly insights from a [SPECIFIC] perspective. Sign up here: [LINK]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[TOPIC/TRENDS]`, `[SPECIFIC]`, `[LINK]`
   - **Example:** "Interested in staying up-to-date with the latest tech trends? Subscribe to my newsletter for weekly insights from a full-stack software engineer's perspective."

10. `P.S. Want to [BENEFIT] like the pros? [PRODUCT/SERVICE] is your secret weapon. Find the link in the comments.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[BENEFIT]`, `[PRODUCT/SERVICE]`
    - **Example:** "P.S. Want to write LinkedIn posts like the pros? AuthoredUp is your secret weapon. Find the link in the comments."

11. `P.S. Ready to [ACHIEVE GOAL] but don't know where to start? Our [SERVICE/PRODUCT] can help you [BENEFIT] with ease. Learn more here: [LINK]`
    - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVE GOAL]`, `[SERVICE/PRODUCT]`, `[BENEFIT]`, `[LINK]`

    - **Placeholders:** `[CONTENT/RESOURCE]`, `[URL]`, `[BONUS]`, `[FUN CLOSING REMARK]`
    - **Example:** "Sign up for free so you don't miss my weekly marketing tips newsletter. To do so, visit my website at [URL]. You can easily find the link on my profile or in the comments. P.S.     - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVE BENEFIT]`, `[PRODUCT/SERVICE]`, `[ACTION - GO TO WEBSITE/SEND ME A MESSAGE/SUBSCRIBE]`
    - **Example:** "Don't miss out on the opportunity to generate more leads and close more sales! Our sales prospecting tool automates the outreach process and provides personalized messages to your prospects. It's the best investment you'll make this year! How it can help you: [LINK]"

15. `Curious about [TOPIC]? Join my free [WEBINAR/WORKSHOP] to learn the ins and outs of [SUBJECT] from an industry expert. Save your spot here: [LINK]`

- Include social proof when available
- Make the CTA clear and easy

### 3. POSITIVE OUTCOME

**What It Is:**
Endings that paint a picture of positive outcomes and benefits. Shows readers what success looks like.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- After aspirational hooks
- When showing benefits
- For solution-aware audiences
- When you want to inspire action through positive vision

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- When urgency is needed
- For problem-focused content
- When readers need specific next steps

**Template Patterns:**

1. `If you're serious about [ACHIEVING SOMETHING], then you need to start [DOING SOMETHING FIRST].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVING SOMETHING]`, `[DOING SOMETHING FIRST]`
   - **Example:** "If you're serious about becoming a thought leader in your industry, then you need to start creating and sharing valuable content."

2. `The key to unlocking [DESIRED RESULT] is [ACTION/STRATEGY]. Don't miss out on this opportunity.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DESIRED RESULT]`, `[ACTION/STRATEGY]`
   - **Example:** "The key to unlocking exceptional team performance is fostering a culture of open communication. Don't miss out on this opportunity."

3. `Elevate your [SPECIFIC AREA] by leveraging [SPECIFIC TECHNIQUE/TOOL]. Experience the [DESIRED OUTCOME] you've been striving for.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[SPECIFIC AREA]`, `[SPECIFIC TECHNIQUE/TOOL]`, `[DESIRED OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "Elevate your outbound sales strategy by leveraging AI-powered prospecting tools. Experience the increased lead generation you've been striving for."

4. `The power of [SPECIFIC STRATEGY] cannot be underestimated. It's the key to achieving [DESIRED OUTCOME] in [SPECIFIC CONTEXT].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[SPECIFIC STRATEGY]`, `[DESIRED OUTCOME]`, `[SPECIFIC CONTEXT]`
   - **Example:** "The power of client case studies cannot be underestimated. It's the key to achieving a higher conversion rate in inbound sales."

5. `This is the secret to [Desired Result].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[Desired Result]`
   - **Example:** "This is the secret to better customer relationships."

6. `Unlock your [DESIRED OUTCOME] by embracing [SPECIFIC ACTION/STRATEGY]. The results will speak for themselves.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DESIRED OUTCOME]`, `[SPECIFIC ACTION/STRATEGY]`
   - **Example:** "Unlock your full potential in outbound sales by embracing social selling techniques. The results will speak for themselves."

7. `[DESIRED OUTCOME] is within reach when you commit to [SPECIFIC ACTION]. It's a game-changer for [SPECIFIC AREA].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DESIRED OUTCOME]`, `[SPECIFIC ACTION]`, `[SPECIFIC AREA]`
   - **Example:** "A 25% increase in sales revenue is within reach when you commit to a balanced inbound and outbound sales approach. It's a game-changer for B2B SaaS companies."

8. `Make sure you determine the potential ROI of [EFFORTS] before committing to a large budget. It could probably be deployed more effectively [ON SOMETHING ELSE THAT MATTERS].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[EFFORTS]`, `[ON SOMETHING ELSE THAT MATTERS]`
   - **Example:** "Make sure you determine the potential ROI of investing in a new CRM system before committing to a large budget. It could probably be deployed more effectively on improving your sales processes."

9. `Invest in [X], and reap the rewards of [ACHIEVING SUCCESSFUL OUTCOME].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]`, `[ACHIEVING SUCCESSFUL OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "Invest in a good landing page, and reap the rewards of better conversions."

10. `Do you want to [STAND OUT/EXCEL] in [AREA]? Implement [ACTION/STRATEGY] and watch the transformation.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[STAND OUT/EXCEL]`, `[AREA]`, `[ACTION/STRATEGY]`
    - **Example:** "Do you want to excel in talent retention? Implement a personalized employee development program and watch the transformation."

11. `Nothing says [GOAL ACHIEVED] like [ACTION]. Give it a try and see the impact for yourself.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[GOAL ACHIEVED]`, `[ACTION]`
    - **Example:** "Nothing says client satisfaction like reduced customer churn. Give it a try and see the impact for yourself."

12. `Ready to take your [KEY METRIC/SKILL] to the next level? Try [ACTION] to see immediate results.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[KEY METRIC/SKILL]`, `[ACTION]`
    - **Example:** "Ready to take your sales game to the next level? Try incorporating storytelling into your pitch to see immediate results."

13. `Transform your [BUSINESS/PERSONAL ASPECT] by [ACTION]. Experience the difference firsthand.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[BUSINESS/PERSONAL ASPECT]`, `[ACTION]`
    - **Example:** "Transform your company culture by prioritizing employee well-being. Experience the difference firsthand."

14. `If you [DO X], [DESIRED RESULT] will follow.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[DO X]`, `[DESIRED RESULT]`
    - **Example:** "If you master the art of networking online, success will follow."

15. `Want to [ACHIEVE GOAL]? Then you need to [DO ACTION]. It's that simple.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVE GOAL]`, `[DO ACTION]`
    - **Example:** "Want to increase your brand's visibility? Then you need to start collaborating with influencers. It's that simple."

16. `[TOPIC] isn't just for [MAIN PURPOSE]. It can also thrive outside of [PRIMARY CONTEXT].`
    - **Placeholders:** `[TOPIC]`, `[MAIN PURPOSE]`, `[PRIMARY CONTEXT]`

**How to Adapt:**

Endings that invite reader participation and engagement. Encourages comments, questions, and interaction.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- When you want to start a conversation
- After engagement-focused hooks
- When building community

- When you need a direct sale
- For formal content
- When you want to avoid comments

6. `What have you found to be true in your experience as [SPECIFIC POSITION]?`

7. `Got any tips of your own for [SPECIFIC TASK/SITUATION]? Share them in the comments!`

### 5. NEGATIVE OUTCOME

**What It Is:**
Endings that show the consequences of inaction or wrong action. Creates urgency through fear of negative outcomes.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- After problem-focused hooks
- When readers need a wake-up call
- For problem-aware audiences
- When urgency is needed

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- For aspirational content
- When readers are already motivated
- For positive brand building

**Template Patterns:**

1. `Let's keep things simple: [ACTIVITY] is complex, don't make it harder than it needs to be.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[ACTIVITY]`
   - **Example:** "Let's keep things simple: Social selling is complex, so don't make it harder than it needs to be."

2. `Stop [COMMON MISTAKE] and start [EFFECTIVE ACTION]. Watch your [KEY METRIC] skyrocket.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[COMMON MISTAKE]`, `[EFFECTIVE ACTION]`, `[KEY METRIC]`
   - **Example:** "Stop micromanaging and start empowering your tech employees. Watch your product team's productivity skyrocket."

3. `When you fail to [IMPORTANT ACTION], you're not just missing out on [POSITIVE OUTCOME], you're also risking [NEGATIVE OUTCOME].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[IMPORTANT ACTION]`, `[POSITIVE OUTCOME]`, `[NEGATIVE OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "When you fail to invest in employee training, you're not just missing out on increased productivity; you're also risking high turnover rates."

4. `If you want to achieve [GOOD OUTCOME], then it's time to stop [BAD THING]. Otherwise, you'll end up [FACING A BAD OUTCOME].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[GOOD OUTCOME]`, `[BAD THING]`, `[FACING A BAD OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "If you want to achieve thought leadership in your industry, then it's time to stop copying your competitors' content. Otherwise, you'll end up blending in."

5. `Ignoring [IMPORTANT FACTOR] could lead to [NEGATIVE OUTCOME]. Don't let it happen to you.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[IMPORTANT FACTOR]`, `[NEGATIVE OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "Ignoring the importance of cybersecurity in your B2B SaaS company could lead to devastating data breaches. Don't let it happen to you."

6. `If you want to [ACHIEVE AMAZING OUTCOME], then make sure you're not [DOING THIS BAD THING]. Otherwise, you're setting yourself up for failure.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[ACHIEVE AMAZING OUTCOME]`, `[DOING THIS BAD THING]`

### 6. PROVOKE

**What It Is:**
Endings that challenge conventional wisdom or provoke thought. Creates curiosity and positions you as a contrarian thinker.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- After contrarian hooks
- When challenging industry norms
- For engaged audiences who like debate
- When you want to stand out

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- For conservative audiences
- When you want to avoid controversy
- For formal content

**Template Patterns:**

1. `The secret to [DESIRED OUTCOME] lies in [UNEXPECTED STRATEGY/TECHNIQUE]. Are you ready to embrace it and transform your [SPECIFIC DOMAIN]`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DESIRED OUTCOME]`, `[UNEXPECTED STRATEGY/TECHNIQUE]`, `[SPECIFIC DOMAIN]`
   - **Example:** "The secret to reducing employee turnover lies in fostering a culture of psychological safety. Are you ready to embrace it and transform your workplace environment?"

2. `Stop wasting your time on [X]. It won't get you anywhere near [Y].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]`, `[Y]`
   - **Example:** "Stop wasting your time on generic email templates. It won't get you anywhere near more conversions."

3. `Think [X] is the key to [SUCCESSFUL OUTCOME]? Think again.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]`, `[SUCCESSFUL OUTCOME]`
   - **Example:** "Think working 80 hours a week is the key to success? Think again."

4. `There's a hidden opportunity in [CHALLENGE/PROBLEM]. Are you willing to uncover it and [ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME]?`
   - **Placeholders:** `[CHALLENGE/PROBLEM]`, `[ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME]`

5. `[COMMON MYTH] is a recipe for failure. Want to succeed? [RESTATE THE OPPOSITE OF THE MYTH]. That's how you'll get the results you're after.`

### 7. SUMMARIZE

**When to Use:**

- For educational social media content
- When you want to reinforce key points

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- When you need a direct sale
- For short content (redundant)
- When you want to create urgency

**Template Patterns:**

1. `So there you have it: [DO X] and [DO Y]. [ACHIEVEMENT] will follow.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[DO X]`, `[DO Y]`, `[ACHIEVEMENT]`
   - **Example:** "So there you have it: Engage in conversations and post valuable content consistently. Your personal brand on LinkedIn will grow exponentially."

2. `In short: [DO X]. [DO Y]. [DO Z]. Result? Guaranteed!`

4. `In a nutshell: Focus on [POINT A], prioritize [POINT B], and remember [POINT C]. Do this, and you'll [ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME].`

## Ending Selection Matrix

**IMPORTANT: These endings are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles, glossary articles, blog posts, or any other long-form content.**

| Content Type | Primary Ending | Secondary Ending | When to Use |
|--------------|---------------|------------------|-------------|
| Social media (problem-focused) | NEGATIVE OUTCOME | SELL | When showing consequences |
| Social media (educational) | SUMMARIZE | SELL | When teaching something |
| Social media (inspirational) | POSITIVE OUTCOME | MOTIVATE | When inspiring action |
| Social media (engagement) | ENGAGE | PROVOKE | When you want comments |
| Social media (promotional) | SELL | POSITIVE OUTCOME | When promoting product |
| Social media (contrarian) | PROVOKE | ENGAGE | When challenging thinking |
| Social media (motivational) | MOTIVATE | POSITIVE OUTCOME | When inspiring action |

## Matching Endings to Hooks

**Problem-focused hooks** (MYTHS, MISTAKES) work best with:
- **NEGATIVE OUTCOME** endings (show consequences of inaction)
- **PROVOKE** endings (challenge to change)
- **SELL** endings (offer solution)

**Aspirational hooks** (INSPIRE, PERSONAL STORY) work best with:
- **MOTIVATE** endings (inspire action)
- **POSITIVE OUTCOME** endings (show benefits)
- **ENGAGE** endings (invite participation)

**Educational hooks** (ADVICE + EDUCATE, EDUCATE) work best with:
- **SUMMARIZE** endings (recap key points)
- **SELL** endings (offer more resources)
- **ENGAGE** endings (ask for feedback)

**Promotional hooks** (PROMOTE) work best with:
- **SELL** endings (direct CTA)
- **POSITIVE OUTCOME** endings (show results)
- **MOTIVATE** endings (create urgency)

**Engagement hooks** (ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS) work best with:
- **ENGAGE** endings (ask questions)
- **PROVOKE** endings (challenge thinking)
- **SUMMARIZE** endings (recap discussion)

## Complete Hook-to-Ending Combinations

### High-Converting Combinations

1. **MISTAKES hook → NEGATIVE OUTCOME ending → SELL ending**
   - Problem → Consequence → Solution
   - Example: "5 LinkedIn mistakes you MUST avoid" → "Ignoring these could cost you clients" → "Get our LinkedIn guide here"

2. **INSPIRE hook → POSITIVE OUTCOME ending → SELL ending**
   - Success story → Benefits → Offer
   - Example: "How I 10x'd my revenue" → "You can achieve the same" → "Learn my system here"

3. **ADVICE + EDUCATE hook → SUMMARIZE ending → SELL ending**
   - Education → Recap → More resources
   - Example: "How to do X" → "Remember: A, B, C" → "Get the full guide here"

4. **PERSONAL STORY hook → ENGAGE ending → SELL ending**
   - Story → Question → Offer
   - Example: "I made this mistake" → "Have you been there?" → "Here's how to avoid it"

5. **MYTHS hook → PROVOKE ending → SELL ending**
   - Challenge belief → Challenge action → Offer
   - Example: "Myth busted" → "Stop doing X" → "Do Y instead (link)"

## Key Takeaway

**CRITICAL: These endings are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles, glossary articles, blog posts, or any other long-form content.**

Match your ending to your hook's emotional journey. Problem-focused hooks need solution-focused endings. Aspirational hooks need positive endings. Educational hooks need summary endings. Most effective social media content uses a hook that creates curiosity and an ending that drives action. Don't be formulaic—adapt these templates to create a complete emotional journey from hook to ending.

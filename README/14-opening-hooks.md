# Opening Hooks

## Purpose
Opening hooks grab attention and keep readers reading. They set the emotional tone and determine whether your content gets read or scrolled past.

**IMPORTANT: These hooks are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles or glossary articles.**

## Core Principle
Your hook's job is to stop the scroll and create curiosity. It should match your reader's awareness level and emotional state. The right hook at the right time can dramatically increase engagement.

**Content Type Restriction:**
- **Do use:** Social media posts (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- **Don't use:** Journal articles, blog posts, glossary entries, or any long-form content

## Quick Decision Guide

### By Reader Awareness
- **Unaware/Pain Aware** → Use MYTHS, MISTAKES, or ADVICE + EDUCATE hooks
- **Solution Aware** → Use INSPIRE, PERSONAL STORY, or ADVICE + EDUCATE hooks
- **Product Aware** → Use PROMOTE, ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS, or EDUCATE hooks

### By Content Goal
- **Build awareness of problem** → MYTHS, MISTAKES
- **Inspire action** → INSPIRE, PERSONAL STORY
- **Promote product/service** → PROMOTE
- **Educate** → ADVICE + EDUCATE, EDUCATE
- **Engage audience** → ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS

### By Emotional Tone
- **Problem-focused** → MYTHS, MISTAKES
- **Aspirational** → INSPIRE, PERSONAL STORY
- **Promotional** → PROMOTE
- **Educational** → ADVICE + EDUCATE, EDUCATE
- **Contrarian** → ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS

## The 8 Hook Categories

### 1. MYTHS

**What It Is:**
Hooks that bust common misconceptions or myths about a topic. Creates curiosity by challenging what readers think they know.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- When readers have misconceptions about your topic
- To differentiate your approach from common wisdom
- For social media content that challenges assumptions
- When you want to position yourself as an expert who knows the truth

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- When the topic doesn't have common myths
- For audiences who are already well-educated on the topic
- When you want to avoid controversy

**Template Patterns:**

1. `[Topic] myths busted.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[Topic]` - Your specific topic
   - **Example:** "Freelance success myths busted."

2. `Don't fall for it - [X] myths about [topic].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "Don't fall for it - 4 myths about launching an online course."

3. `The truth behind [topic] - busting the most common myths.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[topic]` - Your specific topic
   - **Example:** "The truth behind niche influencer marketing - busting the most common myths."

4. `The [X] BIGGEST myths and misconceptions about [topic].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "The 5 BIGGEST myths and misconceptions about growing your company page on LinkedIn."

**How to Adapt:**
- Use specific numbers when possible (creates curiosity)
- Make the myth relatable to your audience
- Ensure you can actually bust the myth in your content

### 2. MISTAKES

**What It Is:**
Hooks that highlight common mistakes people make. Creates urgency and positions you as someone who can help avoid costly errors.

**When to Use:**
- **ONLY for social media posts** (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.)
- When readers are making common mistakes
- To create urgency around avoiding problems
- For problem-aware audiences

**When NOT to Use:**
- **Never in journal articles or glossary articles**
- Never in blog posts or long-form content
- When mistakes aren't actually common
- For audiences who are already experts
- When you want to focus on positive outcomes

**Template Patterns:**

1. `Here's why [topic] isn't working for you.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[topic]` - The activity/strategy
   - **Example:** "Here's why blogging isn't working for you."

2. `[X] reasons you're not [actioning].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[actioning]` - The desired action
   - **Example:** "7 Reasons you're not generating enough quality leads for your pipeline."

3. `The [X] worst [topic] mistakes you can make.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "The 4 worst link building mistakes you can make that can cost you a ton of money."

4. `Top [X] reasons why [topic] is not working for you.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "Top 10 reasons why content marketing is not working for you."

5. `[X] [topic] mistakes you MUST avoid at all costs!`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "5 LinkedIn message mistakes you MUST avoid at all costs (even most people are making them)"

6. `Don't make this [topic] mistake when trying to [outcome].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[topic]` - The activity, `[outcome]` - Desired result
   - **Example:** "Don't make this networking mistake when trying to land a job."

7. `Don't [take action] until you read this!`
   - **Placeholders:** `[take action]` - The action to avoid
   - **Example:** "Don't create an online course until you read this!"

8. `The [X] Worst [topic] Habits To Avoid.`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[topic]` - Your topic
   - **Example:** "The 7 worst tech recruiting habits to avoid if you want the best people."

9. `Here are [X] mistakes I made when [activity].`
   - **Placeholders:** `[X]` - Number, `[activity]` - The activity
   - **Example:** "Here are 4 mistakes I made when I started my first startup."

10. `So many [target audience] get this wrong… Here's the truth.`
    - **Placeholders:** `[target audience]` - Your audience
    - **Example:** "So many digital marketers get this wrong… Here's the truth."

11. `The number 1 mistake [target audience] makes when [performing activity] is [doing X].`
    - **Placeholders:** `[target audience]`, `[performing activity]`, `[doing X]`
    - **Example:** "The No 1 mistake founders make when launching a product is not doing enough market research."

**How to Adapt:**
- Use specific numbers for credibility
- Make mistakes relatable and specific
- Show the cost of the mistake when possible
- Use personal stories ("mistakes I made") for authenticity

- When you want to build trust first

## Hook Selection Matrix

**IMPORTANT: These hooks are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles, glossary articles, blog posts, or any other long-form content.**

| Content Type | Primary Hook | Secondary Hook | When to Use |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Social media (problem-focused) | MISTAKES | MYTHS | When readers have a problem |
| Social media (educational) | ADVICE + EDUCATE | EDUCATE | When teaching something |
| Social media (inspirational) | INSPIRE | PERSONAL STORY | When motivating readers |
| Social media (engagement) | ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS | PERSONAL STORY | When you want comments |
| Social media (promotional) | PROMOTE | INSPIRE | When promoting product |
| Social media (myth-busting) | MYTHS | MISTAKES | When challenging assumptions |
| Social media (personal) | PERSONAL STORY | INSPIRE | When sharing experiences |

## Matching Hooks to Endings

**Problem-focused hooks** (MYTHS, MISTAKES) work best with:
- NEGATIVE OUTCOME endings (show consequences)
- PROVOKE endings (challenge to act)
- SELL endings (offer solution)

**Aspirational hooks** (INSPIRE, PERSONAL STORY) work best with:
- MOTIVATE endings (inspire action)
- POSITIVE OUTCOME endings (show benefits)
- ENGAGE endings (invite participation)

**Educational hooks** (ADVICE + EDUCATE, EDUCATE) work best with:
- SUMMARIZE endings (recap key points)
- SELL endings (offer more resources)
- ENGAGE endings (ask for feedback)

**Promotional hooks** (PROMOTE) work best with:
- SELL endings (direct CTA)
- POSITIVE OUTCOME endings (show results)
- MOTIVATE endings (create urgency)

**Engagement hooks** (ENGAGE + BOLD CLAIMS) work best with:
- ENGAGE endings (ask questions)
- PROVOKE endings (challenge thinking)
- SUMMARIZE endings (recap discussion)

## Key Takeaway

**CRITICAL: These hooks are ONLY for social media posts. Never use them in journal articles, glossary articles, blog posts, or any other long-form content.**

Choose your hook based on your reader's awareness level and your content goal. Match the hook's emotional tone to your ending for consistency. Most effective social media content uses hooks that create curiosity and endings that drive action. Don't be formulaic—adapt these templates to your specific audience and message.

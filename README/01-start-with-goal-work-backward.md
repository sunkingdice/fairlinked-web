# Start with the Goal and Work Backward

## Purpose
Every piece of content should have one clear goal. Start with that goal and work backward to determine what objections, anxieties, and benefits you need to address.

## Core Principle
**One goal per piece of content.** Not one primary goal with supporting goals. Just one goal.

## When to Use
- Writing blog posts: What action should the reader take after reading?
- Writing social media posts: What should this post drive the reader to do?
- Writing glossary entries: What should the reader understand or do after reading?

## The Working Backward Framework

Start with your goal, then work backward through this sequence:

1. **Page Goal** → What do you want the reader to do?
2. **Primary Objection** → What's the main reason they won't do it?
3. **Secondary Objection** → What other concerns might they have?
4. **Primary Anxiety** → What worries them about taking action?
5. **Primary Benefit** → What's the biggest benefit of taking action?
6. **Secondary Benefit** → What other positive outcomes exist?
7. **Counter-objection** → How do you address their concerns?

## Application to Blog Posts

**Goal Example:** Get readers to download a compliance checklist

**Working Backward:**
- **Primary Objection:** "I don't have time to read another checklist"
- **Primary Anxiety:** "This will be too complicated"
- **Primary Benefit:** "Save hours of research with a ready-made checklist"
- **Headline:** "TTPA Compliance Checklist: Save 10 Hours of Research (Free Download)"

## Application to Social Media Posts

**Goal Example:** Get followers to click through to a blog post

**Working Backward:**
- **Primary Objection:** "I've seen this topic before"
- **Primary Anxiety:** "The article will be too long or boring"
- **Primary Benefit:** "Learn the one mistake that costs companies €50K"
- **Post:** "Most companies make this one TTPA mistake that costs them €50K. Here's how to avoid it → [link]"

## Pro Tip: The Prism Principle

When writing your close/CTA, think of your offer as a prism. Shine a light through it and see all the colorful outcomes. Write those outcomes in short bullets near your call to action.

**Example:**
- Download our TTPA guide and you'll:
  - Understand compliance requirements in 15 minutes
  - Avoid costly penalties
  - Get a ready-to-use compliance checklist

## Key Takeaway

Everything in your content should fall naturally out of your goal. Start with the call to action (or desired outcome), and work backward. Don't start by dreaming up a headline and then trying to fill in content that might make people want to complete the goal.

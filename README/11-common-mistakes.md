# Common Copywriting Mistakes

## Purpose
Avoid these four common mistakes that cause content to fail: saying too much or too little, saying nothing, talking to the wrong people, or talking about yourself.

## The 4 Main Mistakes

### 1. Saying Too Much or Too Little

**Too Much:**
- Listing every possible benefit
- Including every feature
- Overwhelming readers with information
- Creating TMI (Too Much Information)

**Too Little:**
- Not addressing key objections
- Missing important benefits
- Leaving readers with unanswered questions
- Failing to provide enough context

**How to Fix:**
- Work backward from your goal to determine what's necessary
- Consider awareness level to determine length
- Include only what moves readers toward your goal

### 2. Saying Nothing Whatsoever

**Symptoms:**
- Vague, abstract language
- Generic marketing speak
- No specific benefits or outcomes
- Fails to address reader's actual needs

**Example of saying nothing:**
- "Our solution is innovative and cutting-edge"
- "We provide exceptional service"
- "Transform your business"

**How to Fix:**
- Use specific, concrete language
- Include measurable outcomes
- Address real problems with real solutions
- Use customer language instead of marketing speak

### 3. Talking to the Wrong People

**Symptoms:**
- Content doesn't match reader's awareness level
- Language doesn't match audience
- Addressing concerns they don't have
- Missing concerns they do have

**How to Fix:**
- Identify your target reader's awareness level
- Use language appropriate for that audience
- Address the objections and anxieties they actually have
- Match content to how they arrived (search, email, social)

### 4. Talking About Yourself

**Symptoms:**
- Leading with company history
- Focusing on features instead of benefits
- Using "we" and "our" excessively
- Failing to connect to reader's needs

**Example of talking about yourself:**
- "We've been in business for 20 years"
- "Our platform has advanced features"
- "We're the industry leader"

**How to Fix:**
- Lead with reader benefits, not company achievements
- Use "you" more than "we"
- Focus on outcomes readers get, not what you offer
- Use customer language to describe benefits

## How to Avoid These Mistakes

### For Blog Posts
1. **Work backward from goal:** Determine what's necessary to achieve the goal
2. **Consider awareness:** Write appropriate length for awareness level
3. **Use customer language:** Listen to how customers talk about solutions
4. **Focus on reader:** Lead with benefits, not company achievements

### For Social Media Posts
1. **Be specific:** Avoid vague, abstract language
2. **Match audience:** Use language appropriate for the platform and audience
3. **Lead with value:** Focus on what readers get, not what you offer
4. **Keep it focused:** Don't try to say everything in one post

### For Glossary Entries
1. **Be clear and specific:** Avoid vague definitions
2. **Use examples:** Show how the term applies in practice
3. **Match search intent:** Address what readers are actually looking for
4. **Focus on education:** Help readers understand, not promote your solution

## Pro Tip: The Polish Trap

Marketers often try to abstract messages to the point that everything can be said in two to six commonly used words. This gives a comforting sense of polish—but polish doesn't convert.

**Instead:** Say something different in memorable ways. Say what your competitors wouldn't dare say. Use specific, concrete language that actually means something.

## Key Takeaway

If you say too much or too little, say nothing at all, talk to the wrong people, or talk about yourself, your content will fail. If you listen to your prospects, write in their language, write toward the page goal (making it their goal), and let a little specificity into your copy, you'll see better results.
